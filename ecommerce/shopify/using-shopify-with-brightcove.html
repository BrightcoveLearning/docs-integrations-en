Introduction

The Brightcove Video Connector for Shopify allows you to upload, manage, and get the embed code for videos within the Shopify store dashboard, making it easy to include videos in your eCommerce solution. These operations are detailed in the sections below.

For instructions on installing the Brightcove Video Connector, see the Overview.

Guidelines tab

In the Guidelines tab you will find instructions for connecting the Brightcove Video Connector to each of the templates you use.

Viewing videos

To view existing videos in your Video Cloud account, click the Videos:

Twelve videos are displayed at a time, and you can navigate through the collection using the forward and back arrows highlighted in the image above.

Filtering the video list

To filter the video list by words that appear in the title, enter those words in the filter box:

You can also filter by tag and or description by clicking More filters:

Note that when you use multiple filters, they are applied with an AND logic. For example, if you enter "box" in the title, tag, and description boxes, only videos that have "box" in both the title and description, and also have the tag "box", will be displayed.

Uploading videos

  1. To upload new videos, click Upload video.
  2. In the Upload Video dialog, enter a Name (required), a description (optional), and tags (optional) for the video.
  3. Click Save.

The video will now be available in Shopify and Video Cloud Studio.

Video options

To access the options for any video click the 3 dots under the video to reveal the options menu:

We will look at these options in sections below.

Edit video

  1. Click the 3 dots under the video and select the Edit Video option in the menu.
  2. In the Video Details dialog, make any changes you want to the Name, Description, and Tags:
  3. Click Save to save your changes.

Delete video

Note that deleting a video is not reversible, and that if the video is associated with any campaigns, it will be missing from that campaign - be sure that this is what you want to do before proceeding.
  1. Click the 3 dots under the video and select the Delete video option in the menu.
  2. In the Video Delete dialog, click Confirm to complete the deletion of the video:

Create campaign for video

Note that you can also create a campaign and associate a video with it under the Campaigns tab.
  1. Click the 3 dots under the video and select the Create campaign for this video option in the menu.
  2. In the New Campaign dialog, add a Name (required), Description (optional) and tags (optional). Also select or change the video:
    Depending on whether you create the campaign from the Campaigns or Videos tab, the video may or may not be prepopulated. You can add or change the video as needed.
  3. Click Save to save your changes.

View live events

To view a list of live events for your account, go to the Live Events tab:

To view a live event in progress (or a recording for a finished event, if one was made) click View:

Add new live event

  1. To add a new live event, click New Live Event to open the dialog shown below.

  2. Fill in the fields:

    • Name (required)
    • Description (optional)
    • Tags (optional)
    • Reference ID (optional) usually used to match an ID in another system.
    • Products (optional) add your product(s) to the live event.
    • Region (required) choose the region that you think will be closest to your target audience.
    • Live Ingest Profile (optional) if you wish to use a different profile than the default one, you can see the renditions created for each profile by selecting it. The profile determines the video renditions actually delivered to viewers (be aware that the actual rendition a viewer sees depends on the bandwidth available on the viewer's connection).
      Note that Live streams must use Live ingest profiles - do not select a profile from the list unless its name includes "Live".
    • Convert event to video asset when complete check the box to save a VOD recording of it. Note that if try to view a finished live event that no recording was saved for, you will see an error displayed in the player.
    • Products click Add Product to open the product selector, where you can filter and select products you want to add to the live event. Click Save when you have made your selections.
  3. Click Save to create the live event.

Viewing analytics

Go to the Analytics tab to view Analytics for your videos:

For more detailed information about the meaning of the various analytics metrics, see the Analytics Report Reference.

There are a number of options here to allow you to filter the data in different ways.

Date range selector

The date range selector gives you a variety of standard date ranges to view analytics for, and also the ability to select your own date or date range:

You can also change the time zone for the reports by clicking the GMT link below the date range selector. The link will take you to Video Cloud Studio, where, if you are an admin for the account, you can change the time zone by following these instructions.

Report type

There are two basic types of reports you can view:

Performance reports
Performance reports provide general information about the performance of the video content such as how many times videos were viewed, what devices they were viewed on, how many seconds of the video was watched, and so forth.
Engagement reports
Engagement reports show you how many views there were at different points in a video - remember that these are views, not viewers, so for example, if someone kept moving the playhead back and rewatched the last few seconds of the video 10 times, that would count as 10 views at that point. The trends can still be revealing, however. A sharp decline in views early in the video, for example, suggests that it is not generally appealing, while a sharp decline later in the video might indicate a boring part or something that suggests to viewers that the video is essentially over.

You can switch back and forth between performance and engagement reports using the report type selector:

Performance report types

When you are viewing performance reports, there are many kinds that you can choose from using the subtype selector to the right of the report type selector:

The different reports are useful in various ways. For example, for your business model, it may be particularly useful to know whether the viewers are predominantly on desktop computers or mobile devices, or which countries most of the views occur it. Similarly, you may want to view data by day, because it is important to you to know if viewers are looking at your campaigns more on weekdays or weekends.

The performance report table below the selectors show the analytics data for indiviual items. It will show up to 10 items at time, and if there are more than 10 items, you will see buttons to navigate through them at the bottom:
